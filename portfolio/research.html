User Research and Questionnaire

Methods, sample design, and statistical evidence

Research Methods

Context Observation

Observed family story-sharing moments around keepsakes to understand real trigger points for interaction.

Semi-Structured Interview

Collected pain points from three generations about preserving memories and maintaining conversation continuity.

Questionnaire Validation

Used a 50-participant survey to validate usability, attractiveness, and communication value.

Prototype Walkthrough

Measured task success rate and completion time through a controlled first-use flow.

Questionnaire Structure (50 Samples)

Scale: 1 (very low) to 5 (very high), recommendation score: 0-10.

Core Questions

  • How easy is the NFC tap / unlock step?
  • How engaging is the 3D memory reveal?
  • How willing are you to leave a reply message?
  • Can you complete the first key task independently?

Background Fields

  • Role (Grandchild / Parent / Grandparent)
  • Age group and story-sharing frequency
  • Task completion time (seconds)
  • Recommendation score and short comment

Personas

Representative users used in design decisions

Mingde Wang

68 | Retired engineer | Grandfather
Technical Comfort

Medium. Uses smartphone and video calls but prefers simple, guided interactions.

Goal

Share meaningful family stories so younger generations remember their roots.

Pain Point

Traditional digital archives feel complicated and not emotionally engaging.

Quote

12 | Student | Grandchild
Technical Comfort

High. Comfortable with mobile interaction, visual content, and gamified flows.

Goal

Learn family stories through playful interaction, not long text explanation.

Pain Point

One-way storytelling is easy to forget and difficult to revisit after family gatherings.

Quote

"If I can unlock stories like a game, I will want to explore more keepsakes."

Journey Comparison

Current pain flow vs. TimeLens-supported flow

Current Flow (Without TimeLens)

1

Find Object

A child notices an old keepsake.

2

Ask Briefly

One short question is asked.

3

Verbal Story

The story is spoken once and fades quickly.

4

No Follow-up

Limited interaction after the moment.

5

Memory Loss

Stories are not preserved or revisited.

Observed gaps:
  • Low engagement for younger users
  • No durable story archive with context
  • Weak intergenerational reply loop

Future Flow (With TimeLens)

1

Tap Keepsake

A tagged object triggers the interaction.

2

Unlock Memory

NFC tap reveals a connected family story.

3

3D Experience

Story is shown with visual depth and emotion.

4

Reply Message

Both generations exchange short messages.

5

Build Archive

Memories remain available for future visits.

Expected value:
  • Higher curiosity and replay value
  • Two-way conversation instead of one-way explanation
  • Persistent memory map for long-term family use
