Evaluation & Reflection

Usability testing, iterative refinement, and final reflection on design implications

Usability Testing

Alpha testing with 3 participants from target demographics

Testing Method

We conducted task-based usability testing with the Alpha prototype on three participants representing the primary stakeholder groups: one grandparent (65+), one parent (bridge generation), and one grandchild (18-22). Each participant was asked to complete four core tasks while thinking aloud.

Tasks Tested

  1. Scan or tap a keepsake to unlock a memory
  2. View the 3D memory reveal and read the story card
  3. Send a reply message to continue the story
  4. Create a new memory entry as a grandparent

Metrics Collected

  • Task success rate (binary: completed / abandoned)
  • Perceived ease of NFC interaction (1-5 Likert)
  • Time to complete each task (seconds)
  • Post-task recommendation score (0-10)
  • Verbal feedback and pain-point observations

Key Findings:
  • Grandchildren completed tasks fastest (avg 52s) and rated NFC ease highest (5/5), confirming the playful, intuitive design for digital-native users.
  • Grandparents struggled most with the initial scan step (NFC ease 2/5), indicating a need for clearer onboarding and larger tap guidance.
  • Parents valued the role-switching feature most, reinforcing that TimeLens succeeds as a communication bridge, not just a storytelling tool.

Iterative Refinement

Before and after changes driven by user feedback and pilot testing

Iteration 1: NFC Tap Area & Modal Stability

Before

The NFC Tools panel had a small tap target on the right side of the screen that frequently caused mis-taps. The modal would also close unexpectedly when users scrolled, breaking the scanning flow. Participant P-A1 (Grandparent, 72) commented: "I tapped several times before anything happened."

Commit: fc9f9cb — "Fix NFC Tools misnavigation and force stable modal opening"

After

We enlarged the tap area and anchored the modal open state so it no longer closes on scroll or accidental edge touches. A visual pulse animation was added to confirm when the device is actively scanning.

Impact: Post-fix pilot testing showed zero mis-tap incidents across 5 additional trials.

Iteration 2: Onboarding Clarity & First-Step Guidance

Before

The scan screen launched directly into the NFC state without explaining what the user should do first. Older participants (P-A1 and S017 in the broader survey) repeatedly requested clearer first-step guidance.

User feedback: "Needs bigger tap guidance for first use." — P-A1

After

Added a contextual instruction card above the scan area explaining the two entry paths (NFC tap or manual fallback). The card auto-dismisses after the first successful interaction so it does not clutter repeat visits.

Impact: Grandparent task-success rate improved from 64% (7/11) in the first dataset to projected 82% based on follow-up walkthroughs.

Iteration 3: Visual Noise Reduction

Before

Portfolio and prototype screens contained explanatory gray subtitle lines on nearly every card. During design review, these were identified as visual clutter that competed with the primary content hierarchy.

Commit: 039a2cd — "Remove explanatory gray subtitle lines across portfolio pages"

After

Subtitles were removed or collapsed into expandable tooltips. The interface now relies on iconography and progressive disclosure to keep the visual surface minimal.

Impact: Cleaner visual hierarchy; cognitive load reduced especially for users with lower digital confidence.

Final Reflection

Social, ethical, and technical implications of the design

Social & Ethical Implications

TimeLens was designed with intergenerational equity as a core principle. By anchoring interaction in physical keepsakes rather than abstract menus, the system reduces the digital literacy barrier that often excludes older adults from family technology.

  • Data privacy: All memory data is stored in LocalStorage only; no server upload or cloud dependency means family stories remain entirely on the user's device.
  • Accessibility: Touch targets exceed 44×44dp, color contrast ratios meet WCAG AA, and every NFC path has a manual fallback for unsupported devices.
  • Inclusivity risk: Users without smartphones or NFC-capable devices are still partially excluded. Future work could explore printed QR codes on keepsake cards as a lower-tech bridge.
  • Emotional safety: Memories can be sensitive. The design avoids public sharing by default and keeps the conversation loop within the family unit.

Technical Reflection — AI Usage

Our team made substantive use of generative AI for code scaffolding, asset generation, and debugging, in compliance with the CPT208 AI policy (PDF p.2). The core design logic and user-research findings remain original group work.

  • Prompts used: Primary prompts for generating the React Three Fiber 3D scene, NFC integration logic, and portfolio CSS layout are recorded in /timelens/ai-logs/.
  • Verification: Every AI-generated component was tested against the user-requirement list (3 must-haves) and cross-checked on both Android Chrome and desktop Safari.
  • Ethical checks: We manually reviewed AI-generated color palettes for color-vision accessibility and verified that placeholder content did not introduce cultural bias.
